TIMAN Group (Advised by: Professor ChengXiang Zhai)

August 2019 - Present

As a member of TIMAN, I was able to work on a multitude of projects focused on studying opioid usage in the US. One of my first ventures included collaborating with Alex Morales, PhD, and UIUC’s HSMG (Health, Social Media, and Technology Group) led by Professor Dolores Albarracín to improve a classifier used to identify actionable messages about opioid use using social media. I designed a novel feature generated from Twitter messages discussing opioid usage by using tweet grammar trees and dependency parse trees to help improve classification models. My efforts on this assignment further inspired me to study how drug usage is discussed and perceived in the US on social media. Thus, for my undergraduate thesis, I developed a ML model with novel features generated from tweeters’ tones and tweet vocabulary to identify significant contexts and topics. The model also works to identify various opinions about drug usage expressed in messages.

Project Junto (Advised by: Professors Mariana Silva & Wade Fagen-Ulmschneider)

August 2019 - Present

Project Junto is an open-source tool that seeks to provide course instructors with an easy means of forming project groups in collaboration-based courses based on a bidding system and a genetic algorithm. As part of this project, I worked on developing the NodeJS application as well as the middleware for storing project data, student bids, and instructor constraints. PURE: Promoting Undergraduate Research in Engineering (Advised by: Rebecca Chen, M.S.)

September 2018 - December 2018

I explored image registration techniques (cross-correlation and mutual information) to identify MRI T-1 and T-2 scans belonging to the same person using TensorFlow. I also performed a comparative analysis of the techniques in complex structures such as MRI scans.

Analyzing & Predicting Chicago Crime

March 2018 - May 2018

I used an EMR Spark cluster to find trends and explain fluctuations over time of a 10-GB dataset on all crimes committed in Chicago over a 20 year period. I also collaborated on developing a predictor model for possible future crimes using time-series analysis techniques and MLlib in Apache Spark.
